## [‚àà](‚àà) Formal Definition & Context

**Composition `‚àò`** is a binary operator that combines two functions (morphisms) `g` and `f` to produce a new function `(f ‚àò g)`. This new function represents the sequential application of `g` followed by `f`.

Within the **Œª‚Å∑ Tensor Field** (`[@L7Tensor.md]`), `‚àò` represents a **specific, fundamental type of tensor interaction**. It's the operation handled by the "Composition Unit" of the `Œª‚Å∑-CPU`, allowing the flow of data/computation through connected morphisms. It takes two existing "vectors" or "derivations" within the `Œª‚Å∑` space and yields a new, composed "vector" (function/derivation).

-   **Formal Definition**: For functions `f: B ‚Üí C` and `g: A ‚Üí B`, their composition `(f ‚àò g)` is a function `A ‚Üí C` defined as `(f ‚àò g)(x) ‚â° f(g(x))`.

## [‚äÉ](‚äÉ) Structure & Properties

-   **Inputs**: Two functions, `f` and `g`, where the codomain of `g` matches the domain of `f`. These functions can themselves be base `Œª‚Å∑` morphisms from `[‚≠ò](‚≠ò.md)` or already composed functions/derivations.
-   **Operation**: The act of chaining. In the `Œª‚Å∑` context, this is a tensor operation `T` where the interaction of `f` and `g` creates a new entity. It maps the output trajectory of `g` directly into the input trajectory of `f`.
-   **Output**: A new, single function that encapsulates the combined behavior of `f` and `g`. This output is itself a valid `Œª‚Å∑` derivation (a vector in the Œª‚Å∑ space) and can be further composed.
-   **Key Properties**:
    *   **Associativity ([‚âÉ](‚âÉ.md))**: `(h ‚àò g) ‚àò f = h ‚àò (g ‚àò f)`. The order of grouping compositions does not affect the final result.
    *   **Identity ([ùüè](ùüè.md))**: The identity function `[ùüè](ùüè.md)` acts as a neutral element for composition: `f ‚àò ùüè = f` and `ùüè ‚àò f = f`.
    *   **Non-Commutativity ([‚áÜ](‚áÜ.md))**: In general, `f ‚àò g ‚â† g ‚àò f`. The order of operations matters.
    *   **Foundation of Derivations**: `‚àò` is how `Derivations` (complex programs) are constructed from individual `Œª‚Å∑` morphisms.
